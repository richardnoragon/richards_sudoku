[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/A_nonomino_sudoku.svg/250px-A_nonomino_sudoku.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:A_nonomino_sudoku.svg)

A nonomino or [Jigsaw](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jigsaw_puzzle "Jigsaw puzzle") [Sudoku](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudoku "Sudoku") puzzle, as seen in _[The Sunday Telegraph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Sunday_Telegraph "The Sunday Telegraph")_

A **nonomino** (or **enneomino** or **9-omino**) is a [polyomino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyomino "Polyomino") of order 9; that is, a [polygon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polygon "Polygon") in the [plane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plane_(mathematics) "Plane (mathematics)") made of 9 equal-sized [squares](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Square_(geometry) "Square (geometry)") connected edge to edge.<sup id="cite_ref-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonomino#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> The name of this type of figure is formed with the prefix [non(a)-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/nona- "wiktionary:nona-"). When [rotations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rotation "Rotation") and [reflections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reflection_(mathematics) "Reflection (mathematics)") are not considered to be distinct shapes, there are 1,285 different [_free_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_polyomino "Free polyomino") nonominoes. When reflections are considered distinct, there are 2,500 _one-sided_ nonominoes. When rotations are also considered distinct, there are 9,910 _fixed_ nonominoes.<sup id="cite_ref-Redelmeier_2-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonomino#cite_note-Redelmeier-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup>

The 1,285 free nonominoes can be classified according to their [symmetry groups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symmetry_group "Symmetry group"):<sup id="cite_ref-Redelmeier_2-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonomino#cite_note-Redelmeier-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup>

-   1,196 nonominoes have no [symmetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symmetry "Symmetry"). Their symmetry group consists only of the [identity mapping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Identity_function "Identity function").
-   38 nonominoes have an axis of [reflection symmetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reflection_symmetry "Reflection symmetry") aligned with the gridlines. Their symmetry group has two elements, the identity and the reflection in a line parallel to the sides of the squares.

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Reflection-symmetric-nonominoes-90-deg.svg/500px-Reflection-symmetric-nonominoes-90-deg.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Reflection-symmetric-nonominoes-90-deg.svg)

-   26 nonominoes have an axis of reflection symmetry at 45° to the gridlines. Their symmetry group has two elements, the identity and a diagonal reflection.

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Reflection-symmetric_nonominoes_45-deg.svg/330px-Reflection-symmetric_nonominoes_45-deg.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Reflection-symmetric_nonominoes_45-deg.svg)

-   19 nonominoes have point symmetry, also known as [rotational symmetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rotational_symmetry "Rotational symmetry") of order 2. Their symmetry group has two elements, the identity and the 180° rotation.

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/C2-Rotation-symmetric_nonominoes.svg/330px-C2-Rotation-symmetric_nonominoes.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:C2-Rotation-symmetric_nonominoes.svg)

-   4 nonominoes have two axes of reflection symmetry, both aligned with the gridlines. Their symmetry group has four elements, the identity, two reflections and the 180° rotation. It is the [dihedral group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dihedral_group "Dihedral group") of order 2, also known as the [Klein four-group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Klein_four-group "Klein four-group").

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/D2_Rotation_and_Reflection_Symmetric_Nonominoes.svg/250px-D2_Rotation_and_Reflection_Symmetric_Nonominoes.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:D2_Rotation_and_Reflection_Symmetric_Nonominoes.svg)

-   2 nonominoes have four axes of reflection symmetry, aligned with the gridlines and the diagonals, and rotational symmetry of order 4. Their symmetry group, the dihedral group of order 4, has eight elements.

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/D4_Rotation_and_Reflection_Symmetric_Nonominoes.svg/250px-D4_Rotation_and_Reflection_Symmetric_Nonominoes.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:D4_Rotation_and_Reflection_Symmetric_Nonominoes.svg)

Unlike [octominoes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Octomino "Octomino"), there are no nonominoes with rotational symmetry of order 4 or with two axes of reflection symmetry aligned with the diagonals.

If reflections of a nonomino are considered distinct, as they are with one-sided nonominoes, then the first and fourth categories above double in size, resulting in an extra 1,215 nonominoes for a total of 2,500. If rotations are also considered distinct, then the nonominoes from the first category count eightfold, the ones from the next three categories count fourfold, the ones from the fifth category count twice, and the ones from the last category count only once. This results in 1,196 × 8 + (38+26+19) × 4 + 4 × 2 + 2 = 9,910 fixed nonominoes.

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Conway_criterion_false_negative_nonominoes.svg/250px-Conway_criterion_false_negative_nonominoes.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Conway_criterion_false_negative_nonominoes.svg)

The two nonominoes which can tile the plane, but cannot form a patch which satisfies the Conway criterion.

37 nonominoes have holes.<sup id="cite_ref-3"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonomino#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonomino#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Therefore a complete set cannot be [packed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Packing_problem "Packing problem") into a rectangle and not all nonominoes have [tilings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tessellation "Tessellation"). Of the 1285 free nonominoes, 960 satisfy the [Conway criterion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conway_criterion "Conway criterion") and 88 more can form a patch satisfying the criterion. Two additional nonominoes admit tilings, but satisfy neither of the previous criteria.<sup id="cite_ref-5"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonomino#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> This is the lowest order of polyomino for which such exceptions exist.<sup id="cite_ref-6"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonomino#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup>

One nonomino has a two-square hole (second rightmost in the top row) and is the smallest polyomino with such a hole.
